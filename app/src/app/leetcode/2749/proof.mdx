# Minimum Operations to Make the Integer Zero â€” Proof

**Operation.** In one step pick $i \ge 0$ and do
$$
n \leftarrow n - \bigl(2^i + \text{num2}\bigr).
$$
Start at $n_0=\text{num1}$. After $k$ steps with indices $i_1,\dots,i_k$,
$$
n_k
= \text{num1} - \sum_{t=1}^{k}\!\bigl(2^{i_t}+\text{num2}\bigr)
= \text{num1} - k\,\text{num2} - \sum_{t=1}^{k} 2^{i_t}.
$$
Let $x := \text{num1} - k\,\text{num2}$.

---

## Theorem (necessary & sufficient)

There exists a sequence of $k$ operations that yields $0$ if and only if
$$
x \ge 0 \quad\text{and}\quad \operatorname{popcount}(x) \le k \le x .
$$

### ($\Rightarrow$) Necessity 

If a sequence reaches $0$, then
$$
0 = n_k = \text{num1} - k\,\text{num2} - \sum_{t=1}^{k} 2^{i_t}
$$

$$
let \text{ x} = num1- *num2 
$$

$$
\;\Rightarrow\;
\sum_{t=1}^{k} 2^{i_t} = x .
$$
Hence $x \ge 0$. Also $2^{i_t}\ge 1$ for each $t$, so $k \le \sum 2^{i_t} = x$.
Among all representations of $x$ as a sum of powers of two (with repetition),
the fewest terms needed is $\operatorname{popcount}(x)$ (use the $1$-bits of $x$),
so $\operatorname{popcount}(x) \le k$.

### ($\Leftarrow$) Sufficiency

Assume $x \ge 0$ and $\operatorname{popcount}(x) \le k \le x$.
Take the binary expansion of $x$ (it uses $\operatorname{popcount}(x)$ powers).
Repeatedly split a term $2^j$ into $2^{j-1}+2^{j-1}$ to increase the term count by $1$
without changing the sum, until you have exactly $k$ terms:
$$
x=\sum_{t=1}^{k} 2^{j_t}.
$$
Applying those $k$ indices gives
$$
n_k=\text{num1}-k\,\text{num2}-\sum_{t=1}^{k}2^{j_t}
=\text{num1}-k\,\text{num2}-x=0 .
$$

---

## Corollaries / quick checks

**C1.** If $\text{num2}>0$ and $\text{num1}\le \text{num2}$, then $x\le 0$ for all $k\ge 1$ (impossible).

**C2.** If $\text{num2}<0$, then $x$ grows with $k$; still need some $k$ with $\operatorname{popcount}(x)\le k \le x$.

**C3.** The minimum operations equal the smallest $k$ satisfying the condition.
